Internship Activities

Internships that lead somewhere.

KIT's internship program is built into every degree — not bolted on. Students rotate through structured placements with industry partners in Cambodia, work on real in-house projects, and qualified graduates can move directly into the Career in Japan pathway with partner employers.

Three ways to intern

Placements that match your program — and your ambition.

Local

Local industry placements in Cambodia

Work with tech firms, design studios, hospitality groups, energy companies, and businesses in Phnom Penh and across Cambodia — on real projects tied to your program of study.

Start your path →
Japan

Career in Japan pathway

A formal pipeline combining Japanese-language preparation, technical training, and partner-company placement — designed to convert qualified KIT graduates into full-time roles in Japan.

Explore the pathway →
In-house

In-house project internships

Build real software, design assets, or research outputs inside KIT's own labs and project teams — a low-friction first internship for students early in their degree.

See the labs →
How it works

From classroom to placement in four steps.

01

Prepare

Build a CV with the career office, attend partner-company info sessions, and complete any required Japanese-language or technical prep.

02

Apply

Submit applications to partner companies or in-house project teams — matched to your program and the Career in Japan pathway if applicable.

03

Intern

Complete a structured placement with mentorship from both a workplace supervisor and a KIT faculty advisor — typically 8–12 weeks.

04

Convert

Strong interns convert placements into return offers — locally in Cambodia or through the Career in Japan pathway with partner employers.

Partner companies & outcomes

Real employers. Real placements.

Placements built around employer needs.

KIT maintains active relationships with partner companies across software, AI/ML, design, tourism, renewable energy, and business — in Cambodia and Japan. Curricula are reviewed with these partners so internship skills map to real job requirements.

For students on the Career in Japan pathway, internships often serve as the bridge between Japanese-language preparation and a full-time placement with a partner employer in Japan after graduation.

FAQ

Common questions about internships at KIT.

When do students start doing internships?

Most students take their first structured placement after their second year, once core program coursework is complete. In-house project internships can start earlier for students who want hands-on experience in their first year.

Are internships paid?

Many partner-company placements in Cambodia and Japan are paid; in-house project internships may be unpaid but offer academic credit and direct mentorship from KIT faculty. The career office works with each student to find a placement that fits their goals and financial situation.
