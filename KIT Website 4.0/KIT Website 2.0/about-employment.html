Why work at KIT

An English-medium campus where teaching connects to outcomes.

Faculty at KIT teach in English to small cohorts and are expected to keep one foot in industry — whether through ongoing practice, consulting, or co-supervising student internships with employer partners. We hire both PhD-holders and experienced practitioners; what matters most is that you can teach and that students get placed.

Staff roles — admissions, career services, labs — sit close to students and to employer partners, with clear ownership of outcomes rather than process for its own sake.

How to apply

Four steps from application to offer.

Our hiring process mirrors our admissions process — simple, fast, and transparent. Most candidates move from initial email to interview within two weeks.

01

Find a role

Match your background to one of the open positions above — or send a speculative application if you teach in a related area.

02

Prepare CV + cover letter

Include a short cover note on what you teach, your English fluency, and any industry or Japan-related experience.

03

Send to careers@kit.edu.kh

Email your CV and cover letter to our careers inbox with the position title in the subject line.

04

Interview

Shortlisted candidates meet the dean and a faculty panel — faculty roles include a short teaching demo. Offers typically follow within one week.

FAQ

Common questions from applicants.

Do I need to speak English to work at KIT?

Yes. KIT is an English-medium university — all programs are taught in English, so faculty must be comfortable teaching and assessing in English. Staff roles that interact with students (Admissions, Career Services) are also English-medium. Japanese language ability is an asset for Career in Japan roles but is not required elsewhere.

Can I apply without a PhD?

Yes. We hire both PhD-holders and experienced industry practitioners. For faculty roles, we weight teaching ability and recent, relevant industry experience at least as heavily as academic credentials. Lab technician and instructor roles are typically practitioner-track rather than PhD-track.
