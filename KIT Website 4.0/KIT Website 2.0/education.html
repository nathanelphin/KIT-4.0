How we teach

Small cohorts. English-medium. Industry-built curricula.

Classes at KIT are intentionally small — students work closely with faculty on project-based coursework, receive regular feedback, and build portfolios that map to real employer expectations. Every program is taught in English, with structured Japanese language support woven into the Career in Japan pathway.

Curricula are reviewed with industry partners each year, so what students learn in the classroom reflects what employers actually hire for — from full-stack engineering stacks to applied ML workflows, service operations, and energy systems.

Academic calendar

Two terms. One summer built for internships.

The KIT academic year runs across two main teaching terms, with the summer reserved for structured internships at partner employers in Cambodia and Japan.

Fall term

August – December

Core coursework begins across all six programs. First-year students build foundations in programming, mathematics, communications, and program-specific fundamentals. Mid-term reviews and project critiques held in October.

View resources →
Spring term

January – May

Advanced coursework, capstone project kickoffs, and structured industry mentorship. Senior students begin Career in Japan language and culture preparation alongside technical electives.

View resources →
Summer term

June – July — Internship period.

Summer is reserved for structured internships at partner employers in Cambodia and Japan — the bridge between coursework and the Career in Japan pathway. Faculty mentors supervise placements and review final reports.
