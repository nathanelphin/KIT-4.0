How to Apply

Apply in four steps.

No IELTS or TOEFL required, no application fee, and no essays to agonise over. Submit your online application, get your documents reviewed, attend a short interview, and receive your offer — typically within weeks.

Start application
The 4 steps

From application to offer.

Most applicants complete the full process in three to four weeks. You can apply from anywhere in Cambodia — interviews are available in person at Koh Pich or online.

1

Submit online application

Complete the application form on the KIT website and upload your high school transcript, ID and a recent photo. Takes about 20 minutes.

2

Document review

The admissions team reviews your file within 7 business days and confirms whether you'll be invited to interview.

3

Interview

A 20–30 minute conversation with faculty — in person at Koh Pich or online. English readiness is assessed here, not by IELTS or TOEFL.

4

Receive offer

Successful applicants receive an offer letter within days, with scholarship decisions and next-step enrollment instructions.

What you'll need

Documents to prepare.

Have these ready before you start — the online form lets you save and resume. All documents can be uploaded as PDF or JPG.

Required

Completed application form

Personal details, contact information, chosen program, and short background questions. Submitted online — no paper form needed.

Required

High school transcript

Official grades from Grade 10 onward, or equivalent. Transcripts in Khmer are accepted — KIT admissions can assist with translation if needed.

Required

Passport or national ID photo

A clear scan of your passport information page or Cambodian national ID. Used to verify identity at enrollment.

Required

Recent passport photo

A clear, recent passport-size photograph (JPG or PNG) for your student record and campus ID card.

Required

Personal statement

A short 300–500 word statement on why you want to study your chosen program at KIT and your career goals — including any interest in the Career in Japan pathway.

Optional

Recommendation letter

One letter from a teacher, employer, or mentor who can speak to your readiness for university study. Not required, but strengthens scholarship applications.

FAQ

Common questions about applying.

Is IELTS or TOEFL required to apply?

No. KIT does not require IELTS, TOEFL, or any other standardised English test. English-medium readiness is assessed during the admissions interview, where faculty gauge whether you can follow coursework delivered in English. Structured English support is available once enrolled if you need it.

When will I hear back after applying?

You'll receive an acknowledgement within one business day of submitting your application. The admissions team reviews documents within 7 business days, and most applicants receive their final offer within 5 business days of their interview — so the full process typically takes 3–4 weeks.
