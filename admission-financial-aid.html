Financial Aid & Scholarships

Scholarships and aid that open doors.

KIT believes cost should not be the reason a capable student stays home. Merit scholarships of 10–25% reward strong academic work, need-based financial aid supports families who can't cover full tuition, and the Career in Japan Pathway Grant backs students committed to working in Japan after graduation.

What's available

Three ways KIT can help fund your study.

Aid can be stacked — a single student may receive both a merit scholarship and need-based aid, up to a capped total. The Career in Japan Pathway Grant is awarded separately and is conditional on a commitment to enter the Japan placement pipeline.

Merit

Merit Scholarship

Tuition reduction of 10–25% based on academic performance in high school and the admissions interview. Awarded automatically with the offer — no separate application.

See tiers →
Need-based

Need-based Financial Aid

Additional tuition reduction for families who cannot cover full tuition, assessed on household income and circumstances. Requires a short aid application with supporting documents.

How to apply →
Pathway

Career in Japan Pathway Grant

A grant for students who commit to entering the Career in Japan placement pipeline after graduation. Covers part of tuition from Year 2 onward, conditional on language and academic progress.

How to apply for aid

Four steps from application to award.

Merit scholarships are awarded automatically with your offer — you do nothing extra. Need-based aid and the Career in Japan Pathway Grant require the additional steps below.

1

Apply for admission

Complete the standard KIT application. Merit scholarship eligibility is assessed as part of your offer — no separate form needed.

2

Submit aid application

For need-based aid, complete the short financial aid form with household income details and supporting documents. For the Japan Pathway Grant, write a short statement of intent.

3

Review & interview

The aid committee reviews your file. Need-based aid applicants may have a brief phone or in-person conversation to verify circumstances.

4

Receive award letter

Your final aid package — combining any merit scholarship, need-based aid, and pathway grant — is confirmed in writing with your enrollment offer.

Merit scholarship tiers

Three tiers of merit award.

Merit scholarships are awarded based on high school grades, the strength of the admissions interview, and (where submitted) recommendation letters. Tiers are reviewed annually — sustained academic performance keeps the scholarship in place.

TierDiscountCriteria
Tier 125%Top academic performers — outstanding high school grades and a strong interview. Top ~10% of admitted students.
Tier 215%Strong academic performers — consistently above-average grades and a clear fit for the chosen program.
Tier 310%Solid academic performers — meet program standards with promise to thrive in the English-medium environment.
FAQ

Common questions about aid.

Who qualifies for financial aid at KIT?

Any admitted student can qualify. Merit scholarships are open to all admitted students based on academic strength. Need-based aid is open to admitted students whose household income and circumstances mean they cannot cover full tuition. The Career in Japan Pathway Grant is open to admitted students who commit to the Japan placement pipeline after graduation.

Do I need to apply separately for scholarships?

For merit scholarships — no. Your admission application is your merit scholarship application, and the award is announced with your offer. For need-based aid and the Career in Japan Pathway Grant, you complete a short additional form after you receive your admission offer.

Ready to begin?
