About KIT

An institute built around one outcome: a global career.

Kirirom Institute of Technology is an English-medium university in Phnom Penh, Cambodia, founded to give Cambodian students a direct, practical route from the classroom into industry — including a dedicated Career in Japan pathway.

At a glance

Three things that define KIT.

History / Mission / Goals

Why KIT exists

KIT was founded to close the gap between Cambodian technical education and the skills employers actually need — teaching every program in English, in small cohorts, with faculty drawn from industry as well as academia.

Mission

Educate. Place. Repeat.

KIT's mission is to deliver English-medium technology and design education in Cambodia that is directly tied to employment outcomes — through structured internships, industry-built curricula, and a formal pathway into careers in Japan for graduating students.

Goals

What we set out to achieve.

Goal

Industry-aligned curriculum

Programs in Software Engineering, AI & ML, Design & Architecture, Tourism, Renewable Energy, and Business are reviewed with employer partners so coursework maps to real job requirements.

Goal

Career in Japan pathway

A formal pipeline — language preparation, technical training, and partner-company placement — connects qualified KIT graduates with employers in Japan.

Goal

Accessible admission

No IELTS or TOEFL requirement, transparent tuition, and merit scholarships of 10–25% keep the path into KIT straightforward for Cambodian students.
