Requirements

What you need to apply.

KIT keeps requirements straightforward — high school completion, a passport or national ID, and the willingness to study in English. No IELTS or TOEFL is required: English-medium readiness is assessed in the admissions interview, with structured support available once you enroll.

Eligibility

Three things you need to be eligible.

KIT welcomes applicants from across Cambodia and abroad. The eligibility criteria are intentionally simple so the admissions decision can focus on fit and motivation rather than paperwork.

Education

High school completion

A completed high school diploma — or equivalent — before enrollment. Grade 12 students may apply with current transcripts and confirm graduation before August.

Age

16 years or older

Applicants must be at least 16 years old at the time of enrollment. There is no upper age limit — KIT welcomes mature students and career changers.

Language

English-medium readiness

All programs are taught in English. Readiness is assessed in the admissions interview — no IELTS or TOEFL is required. Structured English support is available once you enroll.

Required documents

What to upload with your application.

Documents can be uploaded as PDF or JPG. If your documents are in Khmer, that's fine — the admissions team can assist with translation if needed. The recommendation letter is optional but strengthens scholarship applications.

Required

High school diploma & transcript

Official grades from Grade 10 onward, plus your diploma if you have already graduated. Grade 12 students submit current transcripts and confirm graduation before August.

Required

Passport or national ID

A clear scan of your passport information page or Cambodian national ID. Used to verify identity at enrollment.

Required

Completed application form

The online form covers personal details, contact information, chosen program, and short background questions. Takes about 20 minutes to complete.

Required

Recent passport photo

A clear, recent passport-size photograph (JPG or PNG) for your student record and campus ID card.

Required

Personal statement

A 300–500 word statement on why you want to study your chosen program at KIT and your career goals — including any interest in the Career in Japan pathway.

Optional

Recommendation letter

One letter from a teacher, employer, or mentor who can speak to your readiness for university study. Not required, but strengthens merit scholarship applications.

FAQ

Common questions about requirements.

Is IELTS or TOEFL required for admission?

No. KIT does not require IELTS, TOEFL, or any other standardised English test. Because every program is taught in English, your English-medium readiness is assessed during the admissions interview — faculty will gauge whether you can follow coursework delivered in English. If you need to strengthen your English before classes start, KIT offers structured English support in Term 1.

What if my high school grades are low?

Grades are one part of a holistic review — the admissions interview, personal statement, and motivation matter too. If your grades don't reflect your potential, use the personal statement to explain your context and your goals. KIT also offers a foundation term in some programs for students who need additional preparation before the main coursework begins.
